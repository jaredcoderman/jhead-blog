The Effects of Chanting

2025-05-01 · 3 min · Jared Head

As some of you may know I have a deep interest in both meditation and yoga. Specifically yogic philosophy or the “yogic sciences” as they are called. I think a lot of the progress I have found in my life can be attributed to the practices I have learned and developed over time, and I want to spend some time sharing specifically about one of them today.

Mantra#

While I have no credentials, I have learned a lot on my own about different esoteric concepts and so I will give a brief background about mantra and how it works. Then I will get specifically into the mantra “Aum Namah Shivaya”.

The term mantra roughly translates to “instrument of the mind”. It is commonly seen from the outside as chanting some set of syllables repeatedly, for a certain period of time, which may or may not result in an effect on the practitioner. There are a variety of different mantras, some are more potent and focused, some less potent and broad. Yet, in my experience, they all follow basically the same guidelines for if you want them to “work” for you.

Guidelines#

  1. Do not discuss the mantra
  2. Do not change the mantra
  3. Chant the mantra with reverence
  4. Chant the mantra without mind
  5. Let the mantra guide you

Now these are just simply guidelines for having the maximal effect, doing them or not doing them will not make anything “good” or “bad” happen. They just help you avoid certain barriers. However, like most barriers in meditation, these will be bumped into regardless and to be learned by the practitioner themselves.

Now assuming the point of this article is not to “convince” anyone, because that will not work for this kind of thing, I will go into roughly “how it works” or at least how it has worked for me.

I have chanted two different mantras consistently. The first is “Aum Namah Shivaya”, and the second is “Aum Kreem Kalikaye Namah”. Today I will just discuss my experiences with the first.

Etymology#

So assuming we agree that a mantra is essentially a spell which helps you “align” with a certain frequency–A kind of “aspect” of the universe. Let’s look into what “Aum Namah Shivaya” can help move us toward. AUM is the universal sound, it sort of serves as a kind of energetic ignition. NAMAH is “I bow”. SHIVAYA refers to Shiva, not just as a deity, but a representation of the formless, the unchanging, and the dissolution of illusion (usually the false self). When I personally chant it I feel that it is aligning me with the divine masculine within myself.

Personal Effects#

After chanting this mantra on and off for over 6 months, the effects have been vast and now seem to be accelerating. However, broadly they are, confidence, direction, and assertiveness.
